COVID-19
Data Exploration

In this SQL project, I utilized various tools and techniques to explore
COVID-19 data. I employed Joins, CTEs (Common Table Expressions),
Temp Tables, Windows Functions, and Aggregate Functions to manipulate and analyze data.

  • View Project

This SQL project delved into the vast realm of COVID-19 data to gain insights into infection, mortality, and vaccination trends worldwide. A plethora of tools and techniques were employed, showcasing the versatility of SQL in data manipulation and analysis.

The project commenced by filtering data to exclude records with missing continent information. It then dived into comparing the total cases versus total deaths, providing a snapshot of the likelihood of dying from COVID-19 in different countries. The analysis extended to reveal the percentage of a population infected with the virus, offering a profound view of the pandemic's impact.

The investigation continued with a focus on identifying countries with the highest infection rates relative to their populations. Additionally, attention was paid to countries with the highest death counts per population, uncovering the varying impact of COVID-19 across the globe.

To gain a deeper understanding, the data was further broken down by continents, highlighting regions with the highest death count per population. Moreover, global numbers were analyzed to grasp the collective scale of infections and deaths.

Furthermore, the project explored vaccination data, calculating the percentage of the population that received at least one COVID-19 vaccine. Techniques like CTEs and Temp Tables were utilized to enhance the efficiency of data processing.

The culmination of this project involved creating a View to store essential data for potential visualizations, facilitating further insights into the patterns and trends of the COVID-19 pandemic. Overall, this project demonstrated the power of SQL in extracting valuable information from complex datasets, shedding light on critical aspects of the ongoing global health crisis.
